Join the Consortium

Become part of a global effort to advance inflammatory eye disease genomics

Membership is intended primarily for clinicians and researchers who have access to Uveitis phenotyping and/or genomic datasets and want to harmonise and combine their data with similar cohorts for collaborative analyses.

Membership Benefits

Pre-Publication Access

Get early access to results generated by the i3eye Consortium and contribute to ongoing analyses.

Research Collaboration

Participate in the design of experiments to achieve Consortium goals and leverage pooled data.

Data Sharing Network

Access to shared clinical and genomic data from international partners, enabling larger, more powerful studies.

Publication Opportunities

First-author publication rights for discoveries made with Consortium resources; co-authorship on group publications.

Standardized Protocols

Use harmonized phenotyping standards and analytical approaches across all member institutions.

Training & Career Development

Opportunities for junior researchers and students to lead analyses and develop expertise in genomics.

Membership Requirements

  • Academic Institution: Membership is limited to academic institutions and their employees.
  • Memorandum of Understanding: Sign a formal MoU with King's College London establishing collaborative terms.
  • Data Contribution: Willingness to contribute clinical and/or genomic data related to inflammatory eye disease.
  • Phenotypic Standardization: Commitment to standardized phenotyping using SUN criteria and other agreed protocols.
  • Collaborative Engagement: Participation in Consortium meetings, workshops, and regular communication.
  • Confidentiality & Ethics: Maintain confidentiality of shared data and ensure full compliance with data protection and ethics regulations.

Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)

Prospective members are asked to review the joining overview with King's College London, which outlines:

  • Collaborative research goals in inflammatory eye disease genomics
  • Standardized phenotypic and clinical data collection protocols
  • Principles of trust, confidentiality, and open communication
  • Data sharing terms and intellectual property rights
  • Publication policies and citation practices
  • Governance expectations and renewal terms

Note: The joining overview summarises the collaboration framework. Specific collaborations will be governed by separate legal agreements.
